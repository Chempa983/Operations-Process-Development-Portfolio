Context

Problem Context

Why this platform needs to exist.

üî¥ The Problem

  • Citizens often don't know where or how to report issues ‚Äî non-emergency, semi-urgent, or urgent
  • Reports are fragmented across channels, leading to slower response and weak traceability
  • Municipal follow-up is hard to measure without standardized intake and categorization
  • Trust erodes when citizens receive no feedback or timeline after reporting

üéØ The Solution Goal

  • Faster intake with clear ownership and accountability at every step
  • Standardized categorization enabling consistent prioritization
  • Transparent status tracking that keeps citizens informed throughout
  • Data-driven prevention: identify patterns before they become incidents

Core Modules

How the platform is structured ‚Äî four interconnected layers.

01

Citizen App

  • Incident reporting with geo-location + photo/video evidence
  • Category selection: traffic, lighting, crime risk, vandalism, hazards, noise
  • Severity guidance ‚Äî non-urgent vs urgent vs emergency
  • Status tracking: received ‚Üí assigned ‚Üí in progress ‚Üí resolved
  • Location-based local alerts and community updates
Mobile-first Geo-location Evidence upload
02

Municipal Operations Dashboard

  • Standardized intake queue with priority rules and SLA timers
  • Assignment workflows ‚Äî ownership + deadline tracking
  • Evidence review, validation flags, and internal notes
  • Citizen-facing status updates pushed automatically
  • Case closure logic + audit-ready reporting
SLA tracking Assignment logic Audit trail

  • Basic triage guidance ‚Äî what to do now vs wait for response

  • Heatmaps and trend analysis by time, location, and category

Process

Operational Workflow

From citizen report to full resolution ‚Äî every step traceable.

Step 01
Citizen submits report ‚Äî category, severity, evidence, and geo-location captured at intake
Step 02
System validates ‚Äî duplicate detection, incomplete field checks, and basic rule filtering
Step 03
Queue prioritization ‚Äî ranked by severity, risk type, and proximity to sensitive zones
Step 04
Assignment ‚Äî department or partner ownership confirmed, SLA timer starts
Step 05
Execution & follow-up ‚Äî status updates pushed to citizen, internal notes logged throughout
Step 06
Closure ‚Äî resolution code applied, root-cause tagged, prevention suggestion recorded
Step 07
Analytics ‚Äî case feeds KPI reporting and governance trend analysis automatically

Structured for scale ‚Äî from pilot to multi-city deployment.

  • Front-end ‚Äî Citizen app + web-based municipal portal
  • Back-end services ‚Äî Authentication, case management, notification engine
